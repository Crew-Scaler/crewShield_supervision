### Key Research Themes

#### 1. Global Regulatory Harmonization

**2512.02046** - Global AI Governance Overview (31 pages)
Comprehensive analysis of regulatory requirements across multiple jurisdictions, addressing the challenge of training AI systems in compliance with diverse global requirements. Proposes multilayered filtering approaches for regulatory alignment.

**2503.05773** - Between Innovation and Oversight (18 pages)
Cross-regional comparative study examining AI risk management frameworks in EU, U.S., UK, and China. Reveals distinct regulatory philosophies from EU's risk-based model to China's centralized approach.

**2507.11546** - AGILE Index 2025 (81 pages)
Quantitative evaluation framework assessing AI governance across 40 countries using 4 pillars, 17 dimensions, and 43 indicators. Provides standardized methodology for global compliance evaluation.

#### 2. Compliance Framework Implementation

**2503.05937** - Unified Control Framework (18 pages)
Enterprise-focused framework with 42 controls addressing organizational and societal risks. Directly maps to regulatory requirements including Colorado AI Act. Operationalizes governance through structured control implementation.

**2509.11332** - Five-Layer AI Governance Framework (17 pages)
Structured pathway from broad regulatory mandates through standards, assessment methodologies, to certification. Integrates ISO/IEC 42001 with regulatory requirements (EU AI Act, NIST frameworks).

**2412.18670** - Interplay of ISMS and AIMS (12 pages)
Examines integration of Information Security Management Systems (ISMS) with AI Management Systems (AIMS) for EU AI Act compliance. Proposes four new AI modules for BSI IT Grundschutz framework.

#### 3. NIST AI Risk Management Framework

**2403.15646** - NIST RMF Applied to Surveillance Technology (14 pages)
Demonstrates practical application of NIST AI RMF to high-stakes surveillance systems. Develops six-step process for surveillance-specific compliance with NIST framework.

**2401.15229** - Evolving AI Risk Management: Maturity Model (20 pages)
Proposes flexible maturity model derived from NIST AI RMF for evaluating organizational progress on compliance. Addresses the transition from high-level principles to operational "sociotechnical harm mitigation best practices."

#### 4. Healthcare-Specific Compliance

**2504.17669** - HIPAA Compliant Agentic AI Systems (8 pages)
Technical framework for healthcare AI compliance combining:
- Attribute-Based Access Control (ABAC) for granular PHI governance
- Hybrid PHI sanitization using regex patterns and BERT models
- Immutable audit trails for compliance verification

**2509.03286** - Accountability Framework for Healthcare AI (14 pages)
Addresses healthcare accountability as shared responsibility between providers. Establishes compliance mechanisms for joint decision-making in clinical AI systems. Aligns with HIPAA requirements and regulatory expectations.

#### 5. Federated Learning & Privacy Compliance

**2402.05968** - Federated Learning Under EU AI Act (13 pages)
Analyzes how EU AI Act regulations influence federated learning development. Addresses data governance, privacy concerns, and opportunities for regulatory-compliant distributed learning.

#### 6. Risk Taxonomy & Governance

**2406.17864** - AI Risk Categorization Decoded (22 pages)
Comprehensive taxonomy deriving 314 distinct risk categories from:
- 8 government policies (regulatory framework analysis)
- 16 corporate policies
- 4 hierarchical levels: system operations, content safety, societal impacts, legal considerations
Provides quantitative baseline for compliance requirement identification.

**2408.12622** - AI Risk Repository (28 pages)
Exhaustive meta-review extracting 777 risks from 43 taxonomies. Organizes risks using causal framework for compliance-focused governance. Expanded April 2025 to include multi-agent systems risks.

#### 7. AI Certification & Standards

**2408.12289** - Catalog of General Ethical Requirements for AI Certification (15 pages)
Normative and practical guidance for achieving "Trustworthy AI" certification. Addresses six ethical principles:
- Fairness
- Privacy
- Safety
- Sustainability
- Transparency
- Truthfulness

Deliverable from "Zertifizierte KI" flagship project for operational compliance.

#### 8. Organizational Governance & Accountability

**2503.04739** - Responsible AI Systems: Roadmap to Trust (22 pages)
Holistic perspective across four dimensions:
- Regulatory context (EU AI Act emphasis)
- Trustworthy AI technology with standardization
- Auditability and accountability mechanisms
- AI governance frameworks

**2504.18236** - Explainability and Contestability in Public Sector AI (19 pages)
Connects regulatory requirements for explainability and contestability through technical, legal, and organizational perspectives. Identifies individual/collective action pathways for compliance.

#### 9. Ethical Leadership & Compliance Culture

**2410.18095** - Ethical Leadership in the Age of AI (15 pages)
Framework for organizational ethical leadership addressing:
- Governance models including stakeholder representatives
- Compliance culture development
- Ethical implications of AI technology adoption

**2508.03970** - Data and AI Governance for Fairness (8 pages)
Lifecycle governance approach for bias mitigation and regulatory compliance. Addresses systematic assessment and quantification of bias across ML model development.

#### 10. Security Awareness & Training

**2112.06356** - Evaluation of Security Training and Awareness Programs (12 pages)
Best practices for measuring organizational compliance with security training requirements. Addresses effectiveness evaluation of mandatory awareness programs.

**2412.04683** - From Principles to Practice: AI Ethics and Regulations (18 pages)
Examines operationalization of ethical principles (safety, transparency, non-discrimination, traceability, sustainability) for regulatory compliance. Analyzes tensions and complementarities in implementation.

#### 11. Enterprise Risk Management for AI

**2506.04133** - TRiSM for Agentic AI: Trust, Risk, Security Management (32 pages)
Enterprise-scale framework addressing trust, risk, and security in LLM-based multi-agent systems. Incorporates audit modules, security gateways, and privacy management layers for compliance.

**2507.15299** - Overview of Risk-Based Model of AI Governance (20 pages)
Examines risk categorization approaches across Europe, North America, Australia. Recommends drawing on risk governance scholarship for improved regulatory frameworks.

### Governance Model Evolution

The research identifies a progression in AI governance maturity:

**Level 1 - Compliance Awareness**
- Identify applicable regulatory requirements
- Document policies and procedures

**Level 2 - Framework Implementation**
- Implement governance structures
- Establish risk management processes

**Level 3 - Operational Integration**
- Integrate compliance into business processes
- Train organizational personnel

**Level 4 - Continuous Improvement**
- Measure compliance effectiveness
- Conduct regular audits
- Adapt to regulatory changes

**Level 5 - Proactive Governance**
- Anticipate regulatory developments
- Influence industry standards
- Lead ethical AI adoption

### Recommendations for Organizations

Based on the collected research, organizations should:

1. **Establish Multi-Framework Governance**
   - Align with multiple applicable regulations (EU AI Act, NIST RMF, ISO 42001)
   - Create unified control framework (42+ core controls)
   - Implement five-layer governance structure

2. **Implement Risk-Based Compliance**
   - Use standardized risk taxonomy (314+ categories)
   - Categorize AI systems by risk level
   - Apply proportional compliance requirements

3. **Develop Comprehensive Training Programs**
   - Security awareness training for all staff
   - Role-specific compliance training
   - Executive leadership training on governance
   - Continuous refresher programs

4. **Establish Accountability Mechanisms**
   - Document compliance decisions
   - Maintain audit trails
   - Implement explainability controls
   - Create contestability pathways

5. **Monitor Global Regulatory Development**
   - Track changes across 40+ jurisdictions
   - Adapt compliance frameworks
   - Influence standards development
   - Share best practices
